# ContentDocProcess — Standard Operating Procedure

## Purpose
Take a raw idea — a big blob of text, a brain dump, a rough concept — and transform it into a clean, filmable content doc with fleshed-out steps. Simple enough to scan in 30 seconds, detailed enough to film confidently without extra prep. Then publish it as a formatted tab in the master Content Doc Google Doc.

## When to Use
- You have a video idea but it's messy or unstructured
- You've pasted in a wall of text (article, notes, transcript, thread) and need it distilled
- You want a filmable outline with say/show cues for each section

## Input
A blob of text. Can be any of:
- A brain dump of an idea
- An article or blog post
- A transcript or thread
- Bullet points, notes, or scattered thoughts
- A link to a source with context about the angle

**Pay attention to what the user says about the intro.** If they provide specific proof, promises, emotions, or phrasing they want in the SAY THIS intro, use their words as the foundation — don't invent a generic intro. Same for the path/angle — if the user says "I want to show me fixing problems as they come," that IS the path.

## Full Process (9 Steps)

### Step 1: Extract the Core Idea
Read the blob of text and answer:
- **What is this actually about?** (strip away fluff)
- **Who cares?** (why would a viewer click on this)
- **What's the angle?** (tutorial, news, strategy, opinion, walkthrough)

This becomes the ONE-LINER.

### Step 2: Write Titles + Thumbnail Ideas
**Titles** — 5 options. Mix styles:
- At least one "How to..." or "I built..."
- At least one with a number or stat
- At least one emotional/curiosity-driven
- Keep them under 60 characters when possible

**Thumbnails** — 2 concepts. Describe what the viewer would see:
- What's the face/expression?
- What text overlay?
- What visual element makes it pop?

### Step 3: Write the SAY THIS Intro + 4P Framework
This is the hardest and most important part. The SAY THIS intro must:
- Sound natural when spoken out loud (not written/formal)
- Lead with proof (a fact, stat, or undeniable thing that happened)
- Include a promise (what they'll get from watching)
- Be 2-4 sentences max — enough to lock them in, not a monologue
- NOT include framework labels like "Proof:" or "Promise:" — just weave them in naturally
- **Use the user's own words and phrasing when they provide it** — don't sanitize or genericize their voice

Also fill in the 4P FRAMEWORK separately — this is the raw inspiration behind the intro, not something you say on camera:
- **Proof**: The undeniable fact or stat
- **Promise**: What they'll have by the end
- **Problem**: What happens if they don't watch (FOMO, wasting time, falling behind)
- **Path**: The journey of the video (what you'll walk them through)

### Step 4: Build Benefits, Steps + Fleshed-Out Walkthrough

**Benefits** = "Today I'll show you:" followed by 3-4 specific bullets of what the viewer gets, then "So by the end:" with 1-2 sentences summarizing the transformation.
- Be specific: "The exact workflow file that powers this system" not "Learn about automation"
- **Use pain-avoidant framing** — frame benefits as removing pain, not just adding gain:
  - Bad: "Learn how to automate your content pipeline"
  - Good: "Never stare at a blank page wondering what to film"
  - Formula: **[Benefit] without [pain]** or **Don't have to [painful thing] anymore**

**Steps** = the high-level outline with emoji labels, one line each. These become the section headers for the fleshed-out walkthrough below.

**Fleshed-Out Steps** = for each step, write:
- **1 sentence** explaining what this step covers
- **🗣️ Say:** — 2-4 sentences of what to say on camera (natural spoken language, quotation marks)
- **🖥️ Show:** — 2-4 bullet points of what to show on screen (specific and visual)
- **Key point:** — one sentence, the single takeaway

**How detailed should the steps be?**
- Detailed enough to film from without extra prep
- NOT a full script — just say/show cues and the key point
- Think of it as: if the creator reads this step, they know exactly what to do and say without thinking
- The Beginners doc is a good reference for the right level of detail

### Step 5: Build the Quick Glance Card
This is the **last thing you write** but it goes **first in the document** — right after the H1 title, before the 🎯 ONE-LINER. It's a scannable summary that pulls the best bits from the full doc.

**Format:**
```
Title:
[Best title from the titles list — the one you'd actually click on]

Benefits:
* [Benefit 1 — pain-avoidant framing]
* [Benefit 2 — pain-avoidant framing]
* [Benefit 3 — tie to gift if applicable]

Steps:
1 — [Step 1 name]
2 — [Step 2 name]
3 — [Step 3 name]
...

So by the end of this video:
[1-2 sentences: what they'll have or be able to do]
```

**How to write it:**
- **Title**: Pick the single best title from the 5 options
- **Benefits**: 3 bullets, pain-avoidant framing (see below). Pull from the 💎 BENEFITS section but tighten them
- **Steps**: Numbered, pulled from the 📋 STEPS section but shortened to just the name
- **So by the end**: 1-2 sentences, pulled from the "So by the end:" line in Benefits

**Pain-avoidant benefit framing:**
Benefits work best when they remove pain, not just promise gain. Frame each benefit as something the viewer won't have to deal with anymore:
- Bad (gain): "Learn how to automate your content pipeline"
- Good (pain-avoidant): "Never stare at a blank page wondering what to film"
- Bad (gain): "Get free captions on your videos"
- Good (pain-avoidant): "Don't have to spend money on a monthly subscription"
- Bad (gain): "Build an AI show doc system"
- Good (pain-avoidant): "No hunting for ideas, no writing bullet points from scratch"

The formula: **[Benefit] without [pain]** or **Don't have to [painful thing] anymore**

### Step 6: Write the Retention Hook + End-of-Video Gift

**Why Stay to the End** = combines the payoff with the gift:
- Reference the before/after transformation
- Mention the specific free thing they get at the end
- Tie it to the Skool community

**End-of-Video Gift** = every video should have one. Options:
- The workflow .md file (stripped of internal details)
- The exact prompt used in the video
- A template (Google Sheet, Notion, etc.)
- A checklist or cheat sheet

**Safety rules for the gift:**
- NEVER share API keys, tokens, or credentials
- NEVER share Airtable table IDs, base IDs, or doc IDs
- NEVER share internal script file names or code
- ONLY share the process, the template, and the output format
- Strip the "Reference Scripts" and "Publish to Google Doc" sections before sharing

### Step 7: Create Supporting Diagram (Excalidraw)
Every content doc gets **one Excalidraw diagram** — a visual that helps viewers understand how the system/process works without all the complexity. This is a supporting visual the creator can show on screen during filming.

**What to create:**
- A single-page flow diagram showing the key components and how they connect
- Should be understandable in 5 seconds — no walls of text, no deep technical detail
- Think: boxes with labels, arrows showing flow, color-coded by function

**Style guide** (match existing diagrams in `projects/diagrams/`):
- **Blue boxes** (`#d0ebff` fill, `#1971c2` stroke) = core pipeline steps
- **Yellow/orange boxes** (`#fff3bf` fill, `#e67700` stroke) = triggers and human actions
- **Green boxes** (`#d8f5a2` fill, `#2f9e44` stroke) = data sources (Airtable, databases)
- **Purple boxes** (`#f3d9fa` fill, `#9c36b5` stroke) = AI processing (Claude, Gemini)
- **Teal boxes** (`#c3fae8` fill, `#1098ad` stroke) = notifications/delivery
- **Numbered steps** as text inside/beside boxes (fontSize 16, same color as box stroke)
- **Gray arrows** (`#868e96`) connecting boxes with small labels
- **Dashed divider line** to separate automated vs manual sections
- **Title** (fontSize 32) + **subtitle** (fontSize 16, gray) at top
- `roughness: 1` for all elements
- `roundness: { "type": 3 }` for rounded corners on boxes

**CRITICAL — Minimal Excalidraw JSON format:**
The `.excalidraw` file MUST use a minimal element structure or Excalidraw will open it read-only. Use `projects/diagrams/show_doc_pipeline.excalidraw` as the reference template.

Each element should have ONLY these fields (no extras):
```json
{
  "id": "my-element",
  "type": "rectangle",
  "x": 100, "y": 100, "width": 200, "height": 80,
  "angle": 0,
  "strokeColor": "#1971c2",
  "backgroundColor": "#d0ebff",
  "fillStyle": "solid",
  "strokeWidth": 2,
  "roughness": 1,
  "opacity": 100,
  "groupIds": [],
  "roundness": { "type": 3 },
  "seed": 123,
  "version": 1,
  "isDeleted": false,
  "boundElements": null,
  "updated": 1,
  "link": null,
  "locked": false
}
```

**DO NOT include** these fields (they cause read-only mode):
- `frameId`, `index`, `strokeStyle`, `versionNonce`, `autoResize`
- `startBinding`, `endBinding`, `lastCommittedPoint` on arrows
- `containerId` on text (just position text over boxes manually)
- `gridStep`, `gridModeEnabled` in appState

**Arrows** — keep them simple, just `points`:
```json
{
  "id": "arrow-1",
  "type": "arrow",
  "x": 200, "y": 170, "width": 60, "height": 0,
  "points": [[0, 0], [60, 0]]
}
```

**Top-level structure:**
```json
{
  "type": "excalidraw",
  "version": 2,
  "source": "claude",
  "elements": [...],
  "appState": { "gridSize": null, "viewBackgroundColor": "#ffffff" },
  "files": {}
}
```

**File naming and publishing:**
- Save as `.excalidraw` (JSON) in the `projects/diagrams/` folder
- Name: `{topic_slug}.excalidraw` (e.g., `show_doc_pipeline.excalidraw`)
- Upload to Google Drive shared folder (`YOUR_SHARED_DRIVE_FOLDER_ID`) via API
- Set permission: `{"role": "reader", "type": "anyone"}`
- Add the Google Drive link to the 📎 SOURCES / LINKS section of the content doc

**Sizing guidelines:**
- Boxes: ~160-220px wide, ~80-100px tall
- Arrows: ~55-75px between boxes
- Canvas: aim for ~1100px wide, ~450px tall (fits on screen)
- Keep it to 4-7 boxes max — enough to show the flow, not overwhelm

### Step 8: Show the content doc and continue
Show the full content doc in chat, then **immediately continue** to Step 8.5 and Step 9 without waiting for approval. Run the full pipeline **FULLY END-TO-END** in one continuous flow. Only pause if something genuinely fails or if there's an ambiguity that can't be resolved.

### Step 8.5: Generate + Score Thumbnails (4 Systems)
After the user approves the content doc text, generate thumbnails automatically using the Thumbnail Service v2.0. See `skills/THUMBNAIL_SERVICE_SOP.md` for full details.

1. Extract the **best title** from the 🏷️ TITLES section
2. Extract both **thumbnail concept descriptions** from the 🖼️ THUMBNAIL IDEAS section
3. Run the 4-system thumbnail pipeline:
   ```bash
   python3 pipeline/thumbnail_service.py "Best Title Here" --concepts "Concept A description" "Concept B description"
   ```
4. Pipeline produces **18 thumbnails** across 4 creative systems:
   - **System 1 (Viral Videos)**: 3 inspirations × 3 variations = 9 thumbnails
   - **System 2 (Favorites)**: 3 random favorite styles remade = 3 thumbnails
   - **System 3 (AI + Face)**: Face refs + text prompt = 3 unique thumbnails
   - **System 4 (No Face)**: All AI, no face = 3 thumbnails
5. All 18 scored with Must-Click Score (Gemini Flash)
6. Top 3 selected with **system diversity** (prefer picks from different systems)
7. Show the user the top 3 with scores and system labels
8. Wait for user confirmation (or re-generate if needed)

Takes ~5-8 minutes. System 1 runs first (sequential generate → transform), then Systems 2-4 run in parallel.

### Step 9: Publish to Google Doc + Airtable
Once approved, publish the content doc as a new tab in the master Content Doc (Series) Google Doc and create an Airtable tracking record.

**This step has two parts — run them in sequence:**

#### Part A: Add tab + insert content
Use `pipeline/add_content_doc_tab.py` as the reference pattern. The script:
1. Authenticates via `youtube_token.pickle`
2. Calls `addDocumentTab` on the master Content Doc to create a new tab
3. Gets the new `tabId` from the API response (with fallback to re-reading the doc)
4. Calls `insertText` with `tabId` to write all the content into the tab
5. Creates an Airtable record in the Content Docs table

**Tab creation details:**
- **Do NOT specify an index** in `addDocumentTab` — omit it entirely. This appends the tab to the END of the tab list (bottom of the document), which is the desired behavior.
- **Always set a proper `title`** in `tabProperties` (e.g., the video topic name). Never leave it as the default "Tab N".
- The Google Docs API does NOT support `deleteDocumentTab` or `updateDocumentTab` — only `addDocumentTab` exists. If you need to "move" a tab, you must create a new one, copy content, and clear the old one.

**Getting the tab ID:**
- Primary: `replies[0].addDocumentTab.tabProperties.tabId` from the batchUpdate response
- Fallback (if primary returns None): re-read the document with `includeTabsContent=true` and search for the tab by title

**Key details:**
- **Master Content Doc ID**: `YOUR_CONTENT_DOC_MASTER_ID`
- **Airtable base**: `YOUR_AIRTABLE_BASE_ID` (Mate OS)
- **Content Docs table**: `YOUR_CONTENT_DOCS_TABLE_ID`
- **Airtable fields**: `Title` (text) and `Status` (use `📝 Draft` — must include emoji)
- **No `Google Doc URL` field exists** — don't try to write one

#### Part B: Format the tab
Use `pipeline/format_content_doc.py` as the reference pattern. The formatting process:

1. **Read the tab content** and classify each paragraph into one of four types:
   - **h1**: Lines starting with "CONTENT DOC"
   - **h3**: Lines starting with emoji headers (🎯, 🏷, 🖼, 🎤, 📊, 📋, 💎, 🔒, 📎, 🎁, 🔍, 📱, 🔗, 🎬, ⚠️, ⚡) OR exact matches ("Title:", "Benefits:", "Steps:", "So by the end of this video:")
   - **plain** (no bullet disc): "CONTENT DOC", "Let's get started", numbered steps ("1 —", "2 —", etc.)
   - **bullet**: everything else

2. **Clear existing content** from the tab (delete range index 1 to end-1)
3. **Re-insert all text** as one batch insertText call
4. **Apply text styles**: bold on h1 and h3 blocks, center alignment on h1
5. **Apply bullets** via `createParagraphBullets` on all bullet blocks
6. **Re-read the document** to get actual paragraph positions (bullets shift indices)
7. **Apply heading namedStyleType**: `HEADING_1` for h1 paragraphs, `HEADING_3` for h3 paragraphs
8. **Remove bullets from heading paragraphs** using `deleteParagraphBullets`
9. **Make URLs clickable**: find `https://` patterns in the text, apply `updateTextStyle` with `link.url` to make them actual hyperlinks

#### Part C: Embed top 3 thumbnails
After formatting completes, embed the top 3 thumbnails from Step 8.5 into the doc using `pipeline/embed_thumbnails.py`:

```python
from embed_thumbnails import embed_thumbnails_in_doc
embed_thumbnails_in_doc(doc_id, tab_id, result.top_3)
```

**How embedding works:**
1. Finds the end of the 🖼️ THUMBNAIL IDEAS section (the index just before the next heading)
2. Inserts thumbnails in **reverse order** at that index (so they appear in correct visual order)
3. Each thumbnail block consists of (inserted bottom-to-top at the same index):
   - Newline separator (before image)
   - The image on its **own paragraph** (not inline with text) — achieved by having a newline both before and after the `insertInlineImage` call
   - Newline after image
   - Score text: `Must-Click Score: X/10 — [reasoning]`
4. After all images are inserted, re-reads the doc and applies bullet formatting to the "Must-Click Score:" lines

**Image URL format:**
- Primary: `https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/d/{file_id}` (embeddable direct link)
- Fallback: `https://drive.google.com/uc?export=view&id={file_id}` (if lh3 fails)

**Formatting rules:**
- **H1** = the doc title ("CONTENT DOC — [TOPIC]"), centered, bold
- **H3 bold** = all section headers with emoji prefixes (🎯, 🏷, 🖼, 🎤, 📊, 📋, 💎, 🔒, 📎, 🎁, 🔍, 📱, 🔗, 🎬, ⚠️, ⚡) AND exact matches ("Title:", "Benefits:", "Steps:", "So by the end of this video:") AND all step headers (e.g. "🎬 STEP 1 — The Proof")
- **Plain** = items that should be normal text with no bullet disc: "CONTENT DOC" title line, "Let's get started!", and numbered step items ("1 —", "2 —", etc.)
- **Bullets** = everything else — all content items are bullet points
- **Headings must NOT have bullets** — after applying bullets broadly, remove them from heading paragraphs using `deleteParagraphBullets`
- **URLs must be clickable hyperlinks** — find `https://` patterns in the text, apply `updateTextStyle` with `link.url` to make them actual hyperlinks (not just plain text)
- **No fancy styling** — no colors, no backgrounds, no borders. Just H3 bold headers and bullets.
- **Order of operations matters**: (1) insert text → (2) apply text styles (bold, center) → (3) apply bullets → (4) re-read doc for actual positions → (5) apply heading namedStyleType → (6) remove bullets from headings → (7) make URLs clickable. Bullets shift indices, so headings and hyperlinks must be applied by re-reading the doc afterward.

- [ ] STEPS has 4-7 items with emoji labels

## Reference Scripts
- `pipeline/add_content_doc_tab.py` — Adds a new tab to the master Content Doc and inserts content. Handles Google auth, tab creation, text insertion, and Airtable record.
- `pipeline/format_content_doc.py` — Clears and re-formats a content doc tab with H1/H3/bullets. Handles the correct order of operations (styles → bullets → re-read → headings → remove bullets from headings).
- `pipeline/thumbnail_service.py` — 4-system thumbnail pipeline: viral → favorites → AI+face → no-face → score all 18 → select top 3. See `skills/THUMBNAIL_SERVICE_SOP.md`.
- `pipeline/embed_thumbnails.py` — Embeds top 3 thumbnails as inline images into a Google Doc tab's THUMBNAIL IDEAS section.

## What This Is NOT
- This is NOT a full show doc (no hooks, walkthrough markers, transformation goal)
- This is NOT a script (no word-for-word text beyond the SAY THIS intro)
- This is a **content doc with fleshed-out steps** — filmable say/show cues without being a full production doc
- Use `SHOW_DOC_PROCESS_SOP.md` when you need the full production treatment
