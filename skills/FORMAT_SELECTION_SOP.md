# Format Selection — Standard Operating Procedure

## Purpose
Use the Hall of Fame table in Airtable to match trending topics/news to proven viral video formats. This is step 1 of the Show Doc creation process.

## Hall of Fame Formats (Ranked by Avg Outlier Score)

### 1. New Tool Build-Along (9.6x avg) — 4 example videos
**Use when:** A new tool, update, or feature just dropped and you want to show it in action.
**Structure:** Hook (problem/trend, 10%) → Tool Overview (15%) → Live Build/Setup (55%) → Results & Review (15%) → CTA (5%)
**Hook type:** problem_agitation or promise_of_value
**Best for:** Tool launches, new features, setup guides

### 2. Opportunity Strategy Play (8.3x avg) — 3 example videos
**Use when:** There's a big-picture shift, a new market opportunity, or an economic trend worth covering.
**Structure:** Bold macro claim (10%) → Evidence & data (20%) → Framework/Strategy (35%) → Real Examples (25%) → Action Plan (10%)
**Hook type:** bold_claim or shock_stat
**Best for:** Market shifts, "make money with AI" angles, trend pieces

### 3. Advanced Feature Masterclass (5.5x avg) — 3 example videos
**Use when:** A tool your audience already uses just got better, or most people are using it wrong.
**Structure:** Bold unlock claim (8%) → Basics recap (12%) → Advanced deep dive (50%) → Integrations (20%) → Best practices (10%)
**Hook type:** promise_of_value or bold_claim
**Best for:** "Better than 99% of people" angles, power-user guides, plugin/integration showcases

### 4. Deep Dive Report (5.1x avg) — 2 example videos
**Use when:** There's an unfolding story, a comparison to make, or investigative findings to share.
**Structure:** Tease findings (10%) → Background (15%) → Investigation/Analysis (45%) → Key Findings (20%) → Verdict (10%)
**Hook type:** curiosity_gap or demo_tease
**Best for:** Controversies, head-to-head tests, "what really happened" stories

### 5. Contrarian Hot Take (4.1x avg) — 2 example videos
**Use when:** Something is overhyped and you have evidence to challenge the narrative.
**Structure:** Bold negative claim (10%) → What everyone says (15%) → Evidence breakdown (40%) → Real implications (20%) → Alternative/Solution (15%)
**Hook type:** bold_claim or controversy
**Best for:** "X sucks actually", "the truth about Y", debunking hype

### 6. Beginner Breakdown (3.9x avg) — 2 example videos
**Use when:** A new trend/tool is confusing newcomers and there's search demand for "what is X?"
**Structure:** Simple promise (10%) → Plain English explanation (25%) → Why it matters (20%) → Key features (30%) → Getting started (15%)
**Hook type:** promise_of_value
**Best for:** Explainers, "in 6 minutes" videos, onboarding new audiences

## Selection Process

### Step 1: Identify the Topic
Pull the top 3 outlier videos from Viral Radar (last 3 days, sorted by outlier score). The underlying TOPIC of those videos becomes your starting point.

### Step 2: Match to Format
Ask yourself:
- Is this a **new tool/feature**? → New Tool Build-Along
- Is this a **big-picture opportunity**? → Opportunity Strategy Play
- Is this a tool people **already use but poorly**? → Advanced Feature Masterclass
- Is this an **unfolding story or controversy**? → Deep Dive Report
- Can you **challenge the mainstream take**? → Contrarian Hot Take
- Are **newcomers confused** about this? → Beginner Breakdown

### Step 3: Check Hall of Fame Examples
Go to the Hall of Fame table in Airtable, filter by the chosen format, and study:
- The verbatim intros (swipe file for hooks)
- The structural breakdown (section pacing)
- The hook type that worked best

### Step 4: Consider Multiple Angles
The same topic can often work with 2-3 different formats. Pick the one with the highest avg outlier score that genuinely fits. Don't force a Contrarian Hot Take on a tool you actually like.
